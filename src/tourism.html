Visit Our Home Longido

Longido is one among of the six districts in Arusha region of Tanzania with total area 7,885 km square and population of approximately 180,000. It located in northeast with undeveloped and unremarked agroecological resources, landscape and wildlife

Where to Visit?

Cultural tourism-Meet Maasai

Maasai, migrating from Kenya in the 18th and 19th centuries, are the district's predominant group. They lead pastoralist lifestyles, with women building mud-and-grass bomas

men and children tend to livestock. Local leaders and warriors (morani and loiboni) ensure community security. Women also bear the responsibility of fetching water and collecting firewood, vital for sustenance.

Discover the mountains awaiting for your climbing

The region boasts renowned peaks such as Oldonyo Lengai Meru mountain, Longido-Namanga (Kenyan Mountain), Gelai, and Ketumbeini, offering breathtaking vistas and diverse landscapes.

Embark on Lake Adventures: Dive into Nature's Tranquilty

Lake Natron is highlighted as a significant lake in the area which is home to millions of flamingo

Encounter rare and colourful birds

The region teems with colorful birds, including flamingos, major starlings, red and yellow barbets,

masked weavers,beautifull silverbills and secretary birds each contributing to the region's rich avian diversity.

The plains and slopes of the mountains provide a habitat for various animals, such as gerenuks, lesser kudus, klipspringers, giraffes, zebras, gazelles, buffaloes, mongooses, elephants, lions, and silver jackals.

Additional attractions and activities in the area include photographic tourism, hunting blocks, campsites around Lake Natron, viewing of Oldonyolengai active volcano eruptions, the presence of rare mammals in Longido, and game-controlled areas with abundant fauna
