Explore Our Rich History

OSIM FOUNDATION, formed in 2018, is dedicated to enhancing maternal and child health, supporting sanitary pad initiatives, nutritional programs, and empowering women in remote communities of the Arusha region, Tanzania.

The organization focuses on health education, promoting health-seeking behavior, and addressing various dimensions of poverty through socio-economic empowerment programs in marginalized areas.

Meet Pendo, a Maasai girl and founder of OSIM FOUNDATION. She conducted a master research project on birth preparedness and complication readiness among pregnant women in underserved communities of Longido District, Arusha-Tanzania.

Her findings revealed higher maternal and under-five mortalities due to limited health facilities, forcing pregnant women to travel long distances on foot, leading to home deliveries or reliance on traditional birth attendants.

Pendo, having been rescued from cultural constraints, understands the challenges faced by women and girls in the Maasai community, where education for girls is often neglected in favor of early marriages.

During her master's studies in public health, Pendo collaborated with Melfard Sorell, an American, who was moved by the plight of young girls in Maasai families lacking proper menstrual hygiene management.

Many girls dropped out of school due to the embarrassment caused by using makeshift materials. Melfard Sorell decided to sponsor and support the provision of sanitary pads, pants, and soap for these girls. Additionally, they explored other projects to serve marginalized women in remote areas, aiming to promote multidimensional health improvements and inspire hope for a better future.
